- Many business models simply don't work when software engineers cost 200k - 300k per year.
- Most software was never that complex but the act of translating business to code required layers of engineering effort and management
- Most engineers are not actually engineering
- At the top end, models are not even close to replacing senior engineers
  - Judgement, taste, and understanding systems is still too important for real enterprise software

- SWE will truly bifurcate
- At the top end real engineers will steward models with extreme leverage and be paid upwards of millions per year
- At the bottom end most small business, personal, and vertical SaaS will become more like no-code
  - More powerful BaaS products will enable this
  - Personal software becomes more real
